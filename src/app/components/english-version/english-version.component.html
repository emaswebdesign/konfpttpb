The Conference, especially the Anniversary Banquet at the end of the first day, is a chance to make unforgettable acquaintances and collaborations with other enthusiasts of CBT. We are certain that the Conference will be an unusual event for our community!

About the PTTPB

The Zdzislaw Bizon Polish Association for Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (PTTPB) was created in 1998. It is an independent scientific society, associating specialists who actively help people with mental disorders and emotional problems, and engage in scientific work focused on the mechanisms of mental functioning.

In 2000, during the Congress in Granada, the Polish Association for Cognitive Behavioral Therapy was unanimously accepted as a member of the European Association for Behavioural and Cognitive Therapies (EABCT).

It currently counts 1900 members, including 574 certified therapists and 40 supervisors-instructors.

The European Solidarity Center - the co-organizer of the conference

The PTTPB International Jubilee Conference is organized in collaboration with the European Solidarity Center. We encourage you to familiarize yourselves with the history and mission of the European Center of Solidarity. It is an unusual place on the map of Poland, both because of its history and its beautiful architecture.

The project of the Solidarity Museum „Polish paths to freedom” was initiated in March, 1998, from the idea of Paweł Adamowicz, the chairman of the City Council of Gdańsk, and Jerzy Kukliński, PhD in history. Nine months later — on the 29th December 1999 - the Foundation Center of Solidarity was started, with the mission of creating the European Solidarity Center. The Foundation was set up on the initiative of Paweł Adamowicz, president of Gdańsk -with the support of: Lech Wałęsa and the institute named after him, Tadeusz Gocłowski — the Metropolitan Archbishop of Gdańsk, the Independent Self-Governing Trade Union „Solidarity”, the Gdańsk Shipyard, and the authorities of the Pomeranian voivodeship. Bogdan Lis became president of the Foundation.
