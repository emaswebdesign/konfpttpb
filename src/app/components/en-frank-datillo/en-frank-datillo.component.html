Frank Dattilio

Frank M. Dattilio, Ph.D., ABPP is a clinical psychologist and one of the leading couple and family therapists in the world. He is specifically known for his development with the Cognitive-Behavior Therapy model of treatment. He was formerly an instructor in psychiatry with Harvard Medical School for 14 years and is currently a teaching associate. He also maintains a part-time faculty position with the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Pennsylvania Perelman School of Medicine where he has taught for 31 years. Dr. Dattilio has over 300 professional publications and 22 books in cognitive behavior therapy including the areas of couple and family therapy. To date, his works have been translated into 30 languages. He has lectured on just about every continent in the world and his works are used in over 80 countries. He currently serves on the editorial boards of a number of professional refereed journals. Dr. Dattilio has also been the recipient of numerous awards for outstanding achievement in the fields of psychology and psychotherapy. He is also the recipient of the 2013 Outstanding Contribution to Marriage and Family Therapy Award through the American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy (AAMFT).
