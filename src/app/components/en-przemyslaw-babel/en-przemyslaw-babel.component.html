Przemysław Bąbel

Dr hab. Przemysław Bąbel is a psychologist and professor at the Jagiellonian University, where he serves as the chairman of the Discipline of Psychology Council, vice director of the Institute of Psychology, and head of the Pain Research Group. He is also the president of the Polish Society for Behavioural Psychology and a member of the Committee on Psychological Sciences of the Polish Academy of Sciences. Prof. Bąbel is researching the mechanisms of placebo, pain memory, and psychological factors determining pain perception. He is also interested in practical applications of behavioural therapy with children with autism and in treatment of chronic pain, as well as behavior analysis and psychology of memory in education. Prof. Bąbel has authored and co-authored over 80 scientific publications, including eight books, and over 100 popular scientific publications. In 2008, he was awarded the “Złoty Umysł – Mistrz Popularyzacji Wiedzy” (Golden Mind – Master of Popularizing Science) Polish Academy of Sciences Presidential Award, and in 2018, he became the laureate of the Prime Minister’s Award for highly rated achievements being the basis of his habilitation. In 2019, Prof. Bąbel was voted as a fellow of the Association for Psychological Science in recognition of his exemplary contribution to scientific research.
