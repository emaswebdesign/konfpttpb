The Institute of Psychology UJ

The Institute of Psychology at the Jagiellonian University in Kraków (https://psychologia.uj.edu.pl) is the leading psychological research and education center in Poland. The Institute is descended directly from the Experimental Psychology Laboratory—the first of its kind in eastern Europe—founded by Władysław Heinrich in 1903. It is thus the oldest psychological research facility in Poland. The Institute employs over 60 researchers and lecturers, who form seven departments and three centers, as well as 11 research teams. The Institute offers an excellent research infrastructure, consisting of 11 laboratories outfitted with the most advanced equipment. Currently, the Institute is carrying out over 60 research projects financed from external sources, both national and international, totaling up to 60 million PLN. The Institute’s faculty publishes studies in the best international journals. In 2018 alone, they published 62 articles in journals from the ISI Master Journal List. The Institute offers a five-year MA program in psychology. Meeting the EuroPsy standards, the program has also received a commendation from the Polish Accreditation Committee. In 2019, it placed first in the Perspektywy ranking. The Institute also offers the COGNES international doctoral program in cognitive neuroscience (http://cognes.psychologia.uj.edu.pl). The Institute organizes numerous activities to popularize psychology, including, among others, three regular events: The Scientific Coffeehouse (Kawiarnia Naukowa), Science First, and Dose of Science (Nauka w Pigułce, http://lux.psychologia.uj.edu.pl).
